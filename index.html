üìÑOverview

This statement outlines Ufonia's position regarding post-operative cataract refraction data and its availability for the Minimum Cataract Dataset for National Audit (NOD). Our approach is designed to optimise patient care, streamline clinical pathways, and ensure efficient resource utilisation within the NHS, while maintaining robust outcome monitoring and supporting professional development requirements. We aim to provide clarity on how Ufonia balances comprehensive data needs with practical clinical delivery and patient experience.

üí°Our Approach to Data Collection

Ufonia's strategy prioritises patient satisfaction and Patient-Reported Outcome Measures (PROMs), alongside the capability to collect relevant post-operative refraction data for NOD submission. This comprehensive approach is supported by robust evidence.

üéØPROMs as a Priority

We believe the patient's visual function and overall satisfaction post-surgery are critical success indicators. High-quality PROMs data aligns with best practices, emphasising the patient's lived experience.

üìäRefraction Data Accessibility

Refraction data remains accessible via routine General Ophthalmic Services (GOS) sight tests. Patients can choose to share their refractive results directly through Ufonia's SightSnap tool, empowering them and ensuring data privacy.

Our approach avoids duplication of effort, meaning ophthalmologists are not required to undertake additional refraction data collection beyond existing GOS frameworks.

üõ°Ô∏èPatient-Led Sharing

SightSnap facilitates patient-led data sharing, ensuring compliance with the Data Protection Act and giving patients control over their information.

ü§îRationale for Targeted Data Collection

Our rationale for not mandating comprehensive refraction data collection for every patient is informed by evidence, international models, and a focus on efficiency and patient benefit.

üåçInternational Models & Evidence

The Scandinavian model demonstrates effective outcome monitoring through targeted data collection (e.g., one month per year), significantly reducing cost and resource burden without compromising care quality [1, 3].

Modern calibration technology ensures high accuracy in lens selection, and audit data shows minimal need for refractive adjustments in the second eye based on the first.

üèõÔ∏èNOD and NICE Context

While NICE suggests processes for completing the UK Minimum Cataract Dataset [4], participation in the NOD audit is not mandated. The NOD currently does not publish routine refraction data due to insufficient collection [6]. Our approach aligns with these realities, focusing on valuable PROMs and patient-consented refraction data.

üõ†Ô∏èHow It Works: SightSnap & GOS Integration

Ufonia's pathway integrates with existing General Ophthalmic Services (GOS) and utilizes the SightSnap tool to empower patients and provide data for various stakeholders. Here's how it works for different groups:

Patient Journey with SightSnap

  1. Post-Surgery Vision Change: If you experience any change in vision after your cataract surgery, you are advised to seek an assessment from an optometrist.
  2. GOS Sight Test: This assessment is typically covered under a GOS sight test, which includes refraction. This is an existing NHS-funded service for eligible patients [7, 8].
  3. Request Your Data: You are entitled to request your refractive status (prescription) from your optometrist free of charge.
  4. Share via SightSnap (Optional): You can then choose to share a photo of your prescription with Ufonia's Dora platform using the SightSnap tool. This is your choice and helps improve overall care quality and research.
  5. Benefits: Your shared data contributes to your surgeon's understanding of outcomes and helps the wider NHS. You maintain control over your health information.

Surgeon & Appraisal Data

Ufonia's platform provides valuable data for surgeons' appraisals and outcome monitoring:

  • PROMs Data: Dora collects PROMs using Cat-PROM5, creating a change score from pre- to post-operation. This robust data can be used for appraisals.
  • Patient-Shared Refraction Data: Refractive outcomes shared by patients via SightSnap are converted to structured data and returned to providers. This markedly reduces manual data extraction.
  • Combined Insights: Refraction data can be paired with PROM information for a comprehensive view of surgical outcomes.
  • Reduced Burden: No requirement for routine, surgeon-led refraction data collection outside of standard GOS pathways.

Trainee Requirements & Data Access

Trainees can obtain necessary post-operative outcome data for their training requirements:

  • Access to Shared Data: Refractive outcomes shared by patients via SightSnap contribute to the pool of data available for trainees.
  • PROMs for Outcomes: Cat-PROM5 data collected via Dora also provides crucial outcome measures.
  • Sufficient Sample: The combination of patient-shared refraction and PROMs data can help compile the necessary information for training portfolios (e.g., 50 consecutive surgeries).
